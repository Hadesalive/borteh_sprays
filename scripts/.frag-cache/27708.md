#362 in my "Tour de Fragrance"

 I know I was bored 20 Arab fragrance house "Bleu de Chanel" clone attempts ago. Hell I was already bored at Versace Dylan Blue. This is just one of six from Armaf alone I've come across. Aren't you bored too??? So, I almost didn't bother with this and was going to put it straight in the donation box, but I've had it on the shelf for months and thought I should at least review it before giving it away.

 Once past the really screechy, cheap citrus opening, I thought, "Could I actually like this?" It's not close to being a BDC clone. BDC's musk makes for a much calmer experience and no screechy citrus note at all. That makes this scent so much sharper that I think it might appeal to a younger demo than Bleu de Chanel. I gave my Bleu to a 18 year old barista to try out, who returned it and said, "This is kinda sleepy. I think this is more for someone older than me." He meant me of course. And let's be honest, BDC is really tame. Even I thought it was too muted for my taste and the musk was why. So I'm not sure why Armaf made the choice to leave out musk. Maybe just to reduce cost. But as a result this is not a clone, or even a similar IMO. It's a Blue scent of course. But Dylan Blue, Narciso Rodriguez Bleu de Noir, Missoni, all closer to being BDC clones than this and they're not that close. 

 While it's a screechy citrus note in the opening for sure, it's at least not the horrid Kool-Aid/Crystal Light powder Armaf often ruins its scents with and calls "citrus." That crap is not just screechy, but sinus singeing. But I decided to give Tag-Him a few more hours to see what's what, and the screechy went away in about an hour. Completely dry it most resembles a weaker Versace's Dylan Blue, and honestly Dylan Blue is a barely performing second-from-the-bottom-tier scent itself at $50 for 100ml. In any case, I've decided I have all the Blues I will ever use, and even so I was just talked into ordering Bvlgari Tygar yesterday. So yeah, this is okay for what it is, and if I wasn't already swimming in blues I'd probably keep it. It's certainly worth the price I paid. But it's going to the men's shelter.
