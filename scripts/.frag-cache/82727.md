As many reviewers here and on Youtube have noted, this one has a slight harshness in the opening.

 This is my opinion as my short stint as a perfumer: it's not that the ingredients are "cheap" or "synthetic" here, it's just that the top notes aren't perfectly balanced like they are in Sedley. Many clone-houses, I think, make one or two iterations of a perfume and then they run with it, whereas a solid perfume house has a panel that will regulate quality control. 

 Who knows how many iterations of Sedley were made before the final one was accepted, but you can bet there were plenty. You can see this over and over again--Jean Lowe Immortal's unbalanced ginger and methyl pamplemouss opening: the ingredients aren't necessarily cheap--it's just all about the unbalanced blend of them. But those companies will just run with those versions anyway..

 In Maahir: the limey note is a bit too high in the opening; it's dominated by lime and juniper berry, along with the dihydromyrcenol, which also smells limey and is in every man's aftershave and shower gel. It also reminded me of Sprite. At the base I do not detect olibanum as I do in Sedley. 

 Maahir is "prickly" in the opening, but after 20 minutes it gets closer and closer to Sedley. If I had to put a number on it, I'd say 50--60% up close, but maybe 85% in the air.

 There are white florals here that are not listed and I can understand, to some degree, why this was compared to Tommy (even CK One if you are only talking about the floral aspect). After the opening, Maahir Legacy takes on a very dry woody note (resembling ambrocenide) which goes well with the dry martini-like composition. Performance is an issue but then again Sedley isn't a monster projector either. All in all, I would still say Maahir is decent in its own right and I'll give it a 4/5.

 P.S. After smelling the real Sedley, I am amazed that people would pay $300 for it, or whatever the price is. Don't get me wrong, I'd shell out for Naxos or another favorite like Gucci Midnight Stroll. But for me personally, Sedley just isn't outstanding enough for me to warrant the price.
