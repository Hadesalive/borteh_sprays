Yara Moi by Lattafa (often nicknamed "Yara Bianco") is a fragrance that has managed to conquer a solid niche of enthusiasts thanks to its distinctive character, very different from the famous pink progenitor.

 While the pink Yara is a "cloud" of strawberries and cream, Yara Moi veers into warmer, creamier and slightly more sophisticated territories. The Opening (Peach and Jasmine): The opening is juicy but not overly fresh. The peach is ripe and velvety, while the jasmine adds a clean floral touch that keeps the fragrance from becoming too heavy right away. The Heart (Caramel and Amber): This is the soul of the perfume. The caramel here is not "burnt", but more reminiscent of a milk dessert (like dulce de leche). The amber warms the composition, making it enveloping. The Base (Sandalwood and Patchouli): The sandalwood emphasizes the creaminess, while the patchouli in this fragrance is very modern and "clean"; it only serves to give structure and longer wear on the skin, without being earthy.Seasonality: This is a perfect scent for autumn and winter. In the summer heat, the caramel note might be a little too opulent. Durability: Excellent, like most Lattafas. On skin it easily lasts 6-8 hours, while on clothes it can last until the next day. Sillage: Moderate/Intense. It makes itself felt without overwhelmingly "filling" a room, as long as you don't overdo it with the sprays. I recommend it to those who love Gourmand perfumes that don't just smell like sugar, but have a woody and creamy base. If you're looking for a cheaper alternative to niche scents that mix canned fruit and sandalwood, Yara Moi is an excellent choice. It's a creamier, more tropical version (despite no declared coconut) of modern amber fragrances. It is less "adolescent" than the pink Yara and more "feminine“.
