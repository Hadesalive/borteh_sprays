For anyone wondering:

 Is this close to Hawas? 

 Is this a dupe of Hawas?

 No it is not, this is a twist on the Invictus DNA, just like Hawas is but they do not smell the same. 

 Are they redundant to own at the same time?

 If you just want ONE Invictus-like fragrance get the Hawas, that one is more pleasant than this one.

 With that out of the way, this fragrance, just like Hawas, is an inspired by version of Invictus, this opens up around 80-90% similar to current batches of Invictus with a sweeter sort of accord to it, but then it changes dramatically. It gets much leafier and more aromatic, and way more middle eastern.

 It becomes a middle eastern take on Invictus.

 It does smell pleasant, but smelling it is not an eye opening experience, its nice, even calming, because it has a tea-like quality to it, but it isnt the highest quality, its very synthetic smelling, a bit like a car air freshener, dont expect niche levels of quality here. But for what it is, it is really nice, and probably one of the best fragrances for its price point. This one, weirdly enough, I think is less summery than Hawas, Hawas is quite a bit fresher, this works in spring or temperatures under 25C. 

 Performance compared to Hawas is about the same, not much different, but also I dont get monstrous performance with Hawas, around 6H, its fully matured, its a 7 month old bottle, no real changes.

 Conclusion:

 Not Hawas, it does remind of it, but in the same sense that Invictus Aqua or Legend remind of the OG. This and Hawas are similar and take the OG and add a middle eastern twist to it, but really they're different scents.

 I can only describe the scent of this like a sweet lemon drop, mixed with green tea.

 Also dont smell upclose to skin, up close its just an oudy spicy woody synthethic mess. Its supposed to be smelled in the air.
