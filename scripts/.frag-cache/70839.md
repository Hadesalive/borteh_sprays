Opening

 ----------

 Right from the start, there’s a clear grapefruit accord. It’s sour, slightly bitter, but also pleasantly sweet. This isn’t a sharp or irritating citrus, but rather a smooth, fairly juicy grapefruit with hints of amber and woody notes. Unfortunately, this stage doesn’t last very long. The grapefruit fades quite quickly and transitions into a more generic, harder-to-define, yet still pleasant citrus note.

 Drydown

 ----------

 After about an hour, notes of musk, patchouli, and spices start to emerge. What stands out the most is how clearly each individual note can be detected. In many fragrances, notes tend to blend together and become hard to distinguish, but here each one is distinct and easy to pick out, making the overall composition easier to imagine.

 After 5–6 hours, the citrus becomes just a subtle background, while the heart notes take the lead. Up close, the base is still clearly noticeable. Interestingly, the combination of amber and woody notes creates a slightly floral impression, even though there are no typical floral notes listed. It’s quite unusual, but very pleasant.

 The overall scent has a slightly marine character, although it doesn’t really feel like a typical hot-weather fragrance. Despite the freshness, after a longer drydown it works better in cooler conditions. It also has a noticeable soapy quality throughout the drydown, especially up close.

 Performance

 --------------

 Longevity is average. The fragrance lasts, but projection drops fairly quickly and it starts to sit closer to the skin. Application has a noticeable impact on how the scent develops. More sprays bring out the base notes, while fewer sprays emphasize the citrus. Temperature also plays a significant role.

 This can be a bit problematic, as the fragrance tends to change depending on conditions more than it probably should. This is a fairly common drawback of many Middle Eastern fragrances, where small factors can significantly affect performance and perception.

 Versatility

 -----------

 Despite its citrusy opening, this fragrance works better in cooler weather, especially during spring and autumn evenings. It’s not overly sweet or heavy, but it also lacks the lightness typical of summer freshies. It may feel a bit too warm for hot summer days.

 The scent leans masculine, but not excessively so. At the same time, it’s pleasant and safe enough that many people will enjoy it. It’s the kind of fragrance that’s easy to like.

 Overall Impression

 ---------------------

 This is a pleasant marine-citrus composition with well-defined notes and an interesting transition into the base. Even if you’re not a fan of citrus fragrances, this one might still appeal to you, as the citrus here is softer and less aggressive than usual.

 The biggest downside is how quickly the opening fades. The initial freshness and sweetness make the strongest impression, and it’s a shame they don’t last longer.

 Final Thoughts

 ----------------

 The fragrance isn’t perfect and can feel a bit inconsistent at times, but overall it performs well. It has its own character and a few interesting elements that help it stand out among other citrus compositions. If the opening lasted longer and the scent was less dependent on external conditions, the rating would be higher.
